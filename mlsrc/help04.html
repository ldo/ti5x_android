ML-04 — Complex Arithmetic

First, enter complex number X which is the first operand for the remaining operations: enter the real part of X, then press  A , then enter the imaginary part, and press  A  again. Then, enter complex number Y which is the second operand: enter the real part of Y, then press 2nd  A´ , then enter the imaginary part, and press 2nd  A´  again.

(Once entered in this way, the same operand X may also be used for ML-05 and ML-06.)

The remaining functions return the real part of the result in the display, and the imaginary part in the T register. They also replace the X operand with the result, ready for further operations:

 B 
Compute X + Y.
2nd  B´ 
Compute X - Y.
 C 
Compute X x Y.
2nd  C´ 
Compute X ÷ Y.
 D 
Compute XY.
 D´ 
Compute logXY.
 E 
Compute the Xth root of Y.
 E´ 
Interchange the values of X and Y.

Formulas Used

where X = a + bi, Y = c + di (a, b, c, d all real):

X ± Y = (a + c) ± (b + d)i

X × Y = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i

X ÷ Y = (ac + bd) ÷ (c2 + d2) + (bc - ad) ÷ (c2 + d2)i

YX = eX ln Y (Y ≠ 0)

X√Y = e(ln Y) ÷ X (X ≠ 0, Y ≠ 0)

See ML-05 for formulas for complex eX and ln X.

Registers used: 01-06.
