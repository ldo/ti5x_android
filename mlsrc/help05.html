ML-05 — Complex Functions

First, enter the complex number X which is the operand for the remaining operations: enter the real part of X, then press  A , then enter the imaginary part, and press  A  again.

(This is the same initial entry procedure as for ML-04 and ML-06, and indeed the first or only operand entered for any of these programs may be used for any of the others.)

Except for the angular conversion, the remaining functions return the real part of the result in the display, and the imaginary part in the T register, and also replace the X operand with the result, ready for further operations:

 B 
Convert to angular format: shows r, press x⇌t to show θ.
 C 
Compute X2.
 D 
Compute square root of X.
 E 
Compute 1/X.
2nd A´
Compute natural log of X.
2nd B´
Compute eX.

Formulas Used

where X = a + bi (a, b real):

absolute magnitude of X, r = sqrt(a2 + b2)

angle part of X, θ = atan2(b, a)

X2 = r2 cos 2θ + ir2 sin 2θ

sqrt(X) = sqrt(r) cos(θ ÷ 2) + i sqrt(r) sin(θ ÷ 2)

1/X = (a - bi) ÷ (a2 + b2)

eX = ea cos b + iea sin b

ln X = ln r + iθ (X ≠ 0)

Registers used: 01-04.
