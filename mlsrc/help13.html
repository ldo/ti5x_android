ML-13 — Curve Solution

Given an arc of a circle of radius r, angle θ; let the length of the arc be s, and the chord length be c. Given any 2 of these parameters, this program will compute the other 2. It can also compute the sector area and the segment area.

First, enter any 2 of the following:

  • Angle θ (in current angle units): θ  A 
  • Radius r: r  B 
  • Arc length s: s  C 
  • Chord c: c  D 

Now you can determine the remaining 2 unknowns and also the areas:

  • Angle θ (in current angle units): 2nd A´
  • Radius r: 2nd B´
  • Arc length s: 2nd C´
  • Chord c: 2nd D´
  • Sector area A:  E 
  • Segment area a: 2nd E´

Formulas Used

s = rθ (in radians)

c = 2r sin (θ ÷ 2)

A = sr ÷ 2

a = sr ÷ 2 - (cr ÷ 2) cos (θ ÷ 2)

If c and s are the only knowns, then θ is computed by Newton-Raphson iteration. Note this can take some time.

Registers used: 01-06.
